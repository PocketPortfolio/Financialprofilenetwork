## Problem Statement

## Deep Dive / Analysis

The philosophy of instantaneity in financial decision-making transcends mere speed. It embodies the precision, accuracy, and timeliness of data—a trinity that forms the backbone of high-frequency trading operations. At its core, this necessitates a departure from conventional cloud-based applications, which can suffer from latency, to a model that champions **data sovereignty** and real-time synchronization.

### The Latency Conundrum

In a cloud-centric world, data often travels through a maze of nodes, each adding milliseconds of delay. In trading, these milliseconds can equate to a significant opportunity cost. Thus, the latency introduced by cloud applications can be detrimental to the real-time insight required for effective portfolio tracking.

### Sovereignty and Sync

**Data sovereignty** is the principle that data is subject to the laws and governance structures within the nation it is collected. For financial firms, this not only concerns regulatory compliance but also operational autonomy. Freeing your data from the confines of proprietary cloud applications mitigates risks associated with vendor lock-in and grants firms the agility to adapt to market changes instantaneously.
