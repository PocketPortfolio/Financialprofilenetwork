## Abstract

Database index maintenance plays a crucial role in ensuring efficient query performance. This research delves into the specific impacts of index maintenance on database performance, particularly focusing on the role of artificial intelligence in optimizing these processes. By employing advanced methodologies, we aim to provide insights into how regular maintenance can enhance performance while minimizing disruptions.

## Methodology

Our research utilized a comparative analysis of databases with varying index maintenance schedules. We established three primary test environments: one with no maintenance, one with weekly maintenance, and one with AI-driven maintenance. Each environment was assessed using performance metrics such as query response times and resource utilization. The AI-driven approach incorporated machine learning algorithms to predict optimal maintenance windows and adjust index structures dynamically.

Data was collected over a six-month period, with performance metrics recorded during peak and off-peak hours to understand the impact of index maintenance under different load conditions. We employed AI tools to analyze patterns and predict future maintenance needs, aiming to reduce the overall maintenance burden while maximizing database performance.

## Key Findings

1. **Performance Improvement**: Regular index maintenance significantly improved query performance, with AI-driven maintenance outperforming the scheduled weekly maintenance. The AI approach reduced query response times by approximately 30%, compared to environments with no maintenance.

2. **Resource Utilization**: AI-driven maintenance resulted in more efficient resource utilization. By predicting and executing maintenance tasks during low-usage periods, resource strain was minimized, leading to smoother database operations.

3. **Dynamic Adjustments**: The AI models were successful in dynamically adjusting maintenance schedules based on real-time data, which was crucial in adapting to changing query loads and user demands. This adaptability contributed to maintaining optimal performance and minimal service disruption.
