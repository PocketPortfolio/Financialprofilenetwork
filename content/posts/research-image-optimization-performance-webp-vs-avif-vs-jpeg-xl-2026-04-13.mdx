## Abstract

In the realm of web performance, image optimization is crucial for reducing load times and enhancing user experience. This report examines the performance of three modern image formats: WebP, AVIF, and JPEG XL. Each format is evaluated on various parameters such as compression efficiency, quality retention, and decoding speed. The goal is to provide insights into which format offers the best balance for web developers aiming to optimize their sites.

## Methodology

To determine the performance of WebP, AVIF, and JPEG XL, we conducted a series of tests measuring compression ratio, image quality, and decoding speed. Images of various types (photographic, graphic, and mixed content) were compressed using each format's standard settings. The quality was assessed using SSIM (Structural Similarity Index) and PSNR (Peak Signal-to-Noise Ratio), while decoding speed was measured in milliseconds using a controlled environment to ensure consistency across tests.

1. **Compression Efficiency**: Files were compressed to similar quality levels, and the resulting file sizes were compared.
2. **Quality Retention**: SSIM and PSNR values were used to evaluate how well each format preserved image quality.
3. **Decoding Speed**: Measured using custom scripts to quantify rendering times on a typical web browser.

## Key Findings

- **Compression Efficiency**: AVIF consistently provided the smallest file sizes, followed by JPEG XL and then WebP. AVIF's efficiency is largely due to its use of advanced compression algorithms.
- **Quality Retention**: AVIF also excelled in maintaining quality, showing superior SSIM and PSNR scores across various types of images. JPEG XL performed similarly but with slightly larger file sizes, while WebP, although efficient, showed marginally lower quality retention.
- **Decoding Speed**: WebP proved to be the fastest in terms of decoding speed, often completing under 100 ms. JPEG XL was close behind, while AVIF, despite its compression benefits, showed slower decoding times, sometimes exceeding 150 ms.

## Future Trends

As technology and internet speeds advance, image formats will continue to evolve. The focus will likely shift towards formats that offer not just high compression and quality but also faster decoding times. Innovations in hardware and browser technology may alleviate some of the current decoding speed issues seen with AVIF, potentially making it the preferred choice for high-quality web imaging in the future.

## Verdict

While each format has its strengths, the choice between WebP, AVIF, and JPEG XL depends on the specific needs of a project. For rapid decoding and adequate quality, WebP remains a strong contender. However, for projects where file size and quality are paramount, AVIF is currently unmatched despite its slower decoding speed. JPEG XL offers a middle ground, balancing efficiency and speed, making it a viable option for many scenarios.
