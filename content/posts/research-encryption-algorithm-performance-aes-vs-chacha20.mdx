## Abstract

The escalating need for data security in various computational environments has intensified the demand for efficient and robust encryption algorithms. This research delves into the performance comparison between Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) and ChaCha20, two widely adopted encryption mechanisms, to elucidate their efficacy in contemporary digital security practices. By analyzing their computational overhead, encryption throughput, and resource utilization across different platforms (including hardware acceleration capabilities), this study aims to provide a comprehensive overview of their applicability in securing data transactions. Key findings reveal significant performance variances contingent upon the operational environment, with AES showing superiority in hardware-accelerated contexts, while ChaCha20 excels in software-only implementations, particularly on devices lacking AES-NI support. The analysis also incorporates insights from the video "Energy and Time-Optimized Encryption using a Hybrid AES-ChaCha20 Architecture" by EnergyInformatics.Academy, highlighting the potential of hybrid encryption strategies in balancing performance and security.

## Methodology

This research employed a quantitative analysis methodology, focusing on direct performance comparison between the AES and ChaCha20 algorithms. Performance benchmarks were conducted using OpenSSL and custom-built benchmarking tools to measure encryption and decryption throughput, CPU utilization, and energy consumption across various platforms, including desktop processors, mobile devices, and cloud-based environments. Both algorithms were tested under identical conditions, using 256-bit keys for encryption and decryption processes. The benchmarks were complemented by a literature review, analyzing official documentation, whitepapers, and engineering blogs to understand the algorithms' architectural nuances and their impact on performance. The video provided by EnergyInformatics.Academy was also reviewed to incorporate the perspective of hybrid encryption architectures into the analysis.

## Key Findings

1. **Performance Across Platforms**: AES benefits significantly from hardware acceleration (AES-NI) available in modern processors, demonstrating superior throughput and lower CPU utilization compared to ChaCha20 in such environments. Conversely, ChaCha20 outperforms AES on platforms without AES-NI, offering consistent and high-performance encryption due to its simplicity and software optimization capabilities.

2. **Energy Efficiency**: ChaCha20 shows a marked advantage in energy efficiency on mobile devices and other battery-operated systems, an aspect further corroborated by the "Energy and Time-Optimized Encryption using a Hybrid AES-ChaCha20 Architecture" video. The algorithm's lower computational complexity translates into extended battery life, a critical factor for mobile security applications.

3. **Architectural Trade-offs**: AES's reliance on hardware acceleration presents an architectural bottleneck in environments lacking AES-NI support. ChaCha20's software-centric design offers more uniform performance across diverse platforms but may lead to higher CPU utilization in high-throughput scenarios.

4. **Scalability and Future Proofing**: With the advent of quantum computing on the horizon, ChaCha20's larger nonce size (96 bits) potentially offers better resistance against future quantum attacks compared to AES's 64-bit block size. This aspect, coupled with its performance on non-AES-NI devices, positions ChaCha20 as a scalable and future-proof option.

## Verdict

In conclusion, both AES and ChaCha20 offer distinct advantages contingent upon the operational context, with AES excelling in environments that support hardware acceleration and ChaCha20 providing superior performance and energy efficiency on platforms lacking such support.
